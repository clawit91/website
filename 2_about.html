I am a versatile economist and international development expert focusing on effectiveness principles and development financing. My unique strength lies in synthesizing rigorous exploratory data analysis into concise policy recommendations targeted to different audiences. I am passionate about innovative data visualization tools and designs to create engaging reports and studies.

As a freelance data and policy analyst, I have professional experience in the monitoring and evaluation of projects at the macro and micro level, including in-depth questionnaire design and data analysis, research, and drafting of reports, policy briefs and book chapters across diverse topics. I have lived and worked in Cambodia, Uruguay, the USA and Germany successfully collaborated with the following organizations:

  • adelphi, Berlin
  • Center for Development Research, Bonn
  • Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit, Phnom Penh
  • German Development Institute, Bonn
  • GFA Consulting Group, Hamburg
  • United Nations, New York

Motivated by a constant thrive to explore new challenges, I have also volunteered in numerous positions. This includes the co-organization of a panel discussion on blockchain technology and the Sustainable Development Goals in New York, acting as a jury member of Studieren ohne Grenzen, and shaping the scholarship’s council of the Foundation of German Business in its initial years.
