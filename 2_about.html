What motivates a theoretical economist to switch from building models to working in development cooperation? My Eureka moment was when reading an academic paper by Branko Milanovic on global inequality during my studies of economics, in which he claims that a "very large chunk of our income will be determined by only one variable, which generally we acquire at birth: citizenship." Intrigued by this hypothesis, reflecting my own migration Polish background, and motivated to get to the bottom of the cause, I have since combined my economic background and interest in international development to contribute to decreasing inequality.

My holistic overview of international development cooperation is based on insights from an implementing, political and academic angle. Dedicated to creating impact, I have specialized in the monitoring and evaluation of projects at the macro and micro level, including in-depth questionnaire design, intergovernmental outreach and data analysis. Building upon my quick comprehension of facts, I have worked on topics ranging from development financing to transformative processes, and authored reports, policy briefs and book chapters. Successful collaborations and assignments include:

  • adelphi, Berlin
  • Center for Development Research, Bonn
  • Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit, Phnom Penh
  • German Development Institute, Bonn
  • GFA Consulting Group, Hamburg
  • United Nations, New York

I have lived in Cambodia, Uruguay, the USA and Germany, and traveled to more than 40 countries across the Americas, Asia, Africa and Europe. Being already fluent in English, German, Spanish and Polish, I am currently strengthening my French skills. Motivated to explore new challenges and passionate about teamwork, I have also volunteered in numerous positions. This includes the co-organization of a panel discussion on blockchain technology and the Sustainable Development Goals in New York, acting as a jury member of Studieren ohne Grenzen, and shaping the scholarship’s council of the Foundation of German Business in its initial years.
